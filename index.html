AI Generates UI: Adapting to Dynamic Interfaces

AI is increasingly generating personalized UI components and layouts on the fly, moving beyond static templates. Designers must shift from crafting fixed screens to defining dynamic design systems and intelligent rules that guide these generative processes. This allows interfaces to truly adapt to user context and behavior in real-time.

Designing for generative UIs means focusing on the 'how' the interface is built, not just the 'what' it looks like.

Designers need skills in defining constraints, parameters, and feedback loops for AI systems, ensuring generated UIs maintain brand consistency and usability.

Spatial Computing UX: Designing Immersive Realities

The rise of spatial computing (AR/VR/MR) is redefining user experience, moving interactions from 2D screens into 3D environments. Designers must now consider depth, presence, environmental context, and natural human movement to create truly intuitive and immersive digital experiences.

In spatial computing, the environment becomes the interface, and natural human interaction is the primary input.

Develop skills in 3D design, understanding human factors in mixed realities, and prototyping interactive spatial concepts.

New Research Methods for AI-Powered Products

Traditional UX research methods often fall short when studying complex AI systems and their dynamic behaviors. Researchers must adopt new approaches like prompt engineering for user testing, explainability interviews, and long-term behavioral analytics to truly understand user interaction with evolving AI.

Understanding AI's impact on users requires looking beyond traditional metrics and embracing research that unravels dynamic human-AI collaboration.

Experiment with methods for observing and analyzing AI's impact on user trust, mental models, and adaptation over time, ensuring research keeps pace with AI innovation.
